# THIRAMAI Empire — Humanoid Robotics R&D Roadmap (2026)

## Vision

Build a **sovereign humanoid prototyping lane** that reuses **THIRAMAI’s core manufacturing DNA**: extrusion-grade **HDPE** and **PVC** know-how, tooling discipline, and supply-chain relationships with polymer distributors. The goal is not to compete with global OEMs on actuators overnight, but to own **lightweight structural plastics**, **environmental housings**, and **cost-down chassis modules** that integrate with commodity motors and open control stacks.

## Why HDPE / PVC from the pipe line

| Asset | Robotics use |
|-------|----------------|
| **HDPE (PE100 / PE80)** | Impact-tolerant **torso frames**, **hip shrouds**, **battery trays**, cable ducts — low moisture uptake, good chemical resistance for workshop environments. |
| **PVC (rigid)** | **End-effector shells**, **sensor mounts**, **joint covers** where stiffness and flame ratings matter; suitable for fixed indoor cells before migrating to higher-performance polymers. |
| **Extrusion + cut-off scrap** | Feedstock path to **shredding → filament or pellet** for **FDM 3D printing** of brackets and one-off humanoid parts (see cross-industry scrap loop in strategy briefs). |

## 2026 phased roadmap

1. **Q1–Q2 — Materials lab & CAD library**  
   Standardize wall thickness, rib patterns, and snap-fit interfaces for HDPE/PVC sheet and profile parts. Publish an internal **“Empire Plastics Kit”** for humanoid-scale modules (200–500 mm characteristic length). **Detailed CAD v1:** [`humanoid_design_v1.md`](./humanoid_design_v1.md) (scrap chassis, printable joints, metric dimensions).

2. **Q2–Q3 — Integration prototypes**  
   Pair printed/extruded structures with **OTS actuators** (BLDC + harmonic or belt reductions). Target: **biped or mobile manipulator torso** demonstrator with **sovereign-sourced** plastic shell >60% by mass (excluding motors/battery).

3. **Q4 — Field hardening**  
   Vibration, drop, and thermal cycling on shop floor; document **recycling loop** from pipe-line scrap into print stock with traceability (batch IDs, MFI bands).

## Dependencies

- Stable **extrusion line uptime** (hydraulic / extruder health).  
- **CRM / GST** rhythm restored so R&D spend is auditable.  
- Optional partnerships: universities, fab labs, or regional **robotics clubs** for control software — THIRAMAI retains **mechanical sovereignty**.

## Success metrics (illustrative)

- **≥3** humanoid-relevant plastic part families qualified for production repeatability.  
- **≥1** closed-loop demo: **line scrap → processed granulate → printed bracket** on a sovereign fixture.  
- Zero conflict with **food-grade or potable** resin lanes — robotics uses **clearly separated** color/additive batches.
