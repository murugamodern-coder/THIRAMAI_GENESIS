# THIRAMAI Empire — Solar × Agriculture Integration (2026)

## Vision

Use **on-site and edge-of-factory solar** to **stabilize energy cost** for **HDPE/PVC extrusion** and to power **adjacent agri-tech pilots** (drip infrastructure, cold-chain edges, pump controllers). The Sovereign thesis: **pipe manufacturing** remains the cash engine; **solar** reduces OPEX volatility and opens **green positioning** for export-facing customers and ESG-minded buyers.

## Synergies with pipe manufacturing

| Lever | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Roof / yard PV** | Offset **extruder motor and chiller** load during daylight; size for **partial** coverage first, then expand with production telemetry. |
| **Thermal coupling** | Waste heat from process (where recoverable) + solar-driven **daytime** peak shaving — document in plant energy balance (Illustrative until meters exist). |
| **Product line** | **Solar-ready mounting** for agri pipe racks, **UV-stable** HDPE grades for outdoor conduit — cross-sell with existing distributor channels. |

## Agri integration (non-competing with core pipe SKU)

- **Microgrids for pump sets** serving the same regions where THIRAMAI pipe is already specified.  
- **Drip / lateral** compatibility: ensure **solar-powered controller enclosures** use **in-house plastic** (PVC/HDPE) for IP-rated boxes.  
- **Data sovereignty**: log **kWh vs. kg produced** for future **carbon-intensity per ton** narrative (marketing + compliance optionality).

## 2026 milestones

1. **Energy audit baseline** — extrusion kWh/ton, demand profile, and roof suitability (desktop + site).  
2. **Pilot array** — target **100–300 kWp** class (indicative) behind-the-meter, subject to DISCOM rules and capex guardrails.  
3. **Agri pilot bundle** — one **reference installation** combining THIRAMAI pipe + solar-powered pump skid (partnership or sovereign demo).

## Links to humanoid R&D

Shared **power electronics enclosures**, **cable management**, and **outdoor-rated** plastic parts can serve **both** solar field hardware and **robotics outdoor trials** — one **Empire plastics** stack, multiple markets.

## Risks

- Grid connection timelines and **net metering** policy shifts.  
- Capex competition with **hydraulic repair** and raw-material floats — **procurement advice** from market_watch should inform resin buys before solar expansion.
